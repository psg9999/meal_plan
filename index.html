Protein, every meal

Eggs, dairy, meat, or beans at breakfast, lunch, and dinner — growing, training bodies need more than a sedentary teen's plate.

Weight-gain fuel

The 16-year-old aiming to gain weight should lean on calorie-dense meals, extra snacks, and consistent protein at each meal, while the 11-year-old playing football, basketball, and baseball needs hearty breakfasts and bigger carb portions before practices.

Carbs are fuel, not filler

Rice, pasta, potatoes, oats, and bread are the primary energy source for practices and games. Don't cut portions before hard training days.

Hydration

Water bottle refilled throughout the day, with extra during two-a-days or tournament weekends.

Iron, for the 14-year-old

Athletic girls run a higher risk of iron deficiency, especially post-menarche. Lean on red meat, spinach, and beans a few times a week.

Pre-practice snack

Something with quick carbs and a little protein, 60–90 minutes out: banana with peanut butter, or a granola bar with milk.

Post-practice recovery

Chocolate milk or a protein smoothie within 30–45 minutes of finishing hits the carb-to-protein ratio that speeds recovery.
